Condoms

History of Condoms

Condoms have been used for centuries as a form of contraception and protection against sexually transmitted infections (STIs). They have evolved over time, with early versions made from materials like animal intestines and later advancements using latex or polyurethane. Condoms provide a barrier that prevents sperm from entering the vagina and reduce the risk of STIs by preventing contact between the genital areas. Today, condoms are widely available in various sizes, textures, and flavors, offering individuals a safe and effective method of protection.

Contraceptives

Benefits of Contraceptives

Contraceptives are a range of methods and devices designed to prevent pregnancy. They offer several benefits to individuals and couples. Contraceptives provide control over family planning, allowing individuals to choose if and when to have children, promoting reproductive autonomy. They can help prevent unintended pregnancies, reducing the need for abortion and the associated risks. Additionally, some contraceptives offer non-contraceptive benefits, such as regulating menstrual cycles, reducing menstrual pain, and managing hormonal imbalances. Contraceptives play a vital role in promoting sexual and reproductive health, empowering individuals to make informed decisions about their bodies and futures.
